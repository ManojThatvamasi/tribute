Early Years (1949-1969)

  • 1949 (February 12) : : Gundappa Viswanath is born in Bangalore, India.
  • 1950s-1960s : : Viswanath shows early promise in cricket, playing street cricket and in school teams.
  • 1967 : : Makes his first-class debut for Mysore (now Karnataka) against Andhra Pradesh, scoring a half-century.
  • 1969 : : Makes his Test debut for India against Australia in Kanpur on January 22, scoring 137 runs.

Cricketing Career (1970s-1980s)

  • 1970-1983 : : Viswanath becomes a key figure in the Indian cricket team, known for his stylish batting technique and elegant strokeplay.
  • 1971 : : Plays a crucial role in India's historic Test series win against the West Indies in the Caribbean, scoring vital runs.
  • 1975: : Scores a memorable century against England at Lord's, helping India to a famous victory.
  • 1976-1977 : : Captains India in Test matches against New Zealand and England.
  • 1979 : : Leads India to victory in the World Series Cricket tournament in Australia.
  • 1980 : : Scores his last Test century against Australia in Chennai.
  • 1983 : : Retires from international cricket after playing 91 Test matches, scoring 6,080 runs at an average of 41.93.

Post-Retirement Contributions (1980s-Present)

  • 1988 : : Becomes a national selector for the Indian cricket team.
  • 1990s-2000s : : Serves as the manager of the Indian cricket team on several occasions.
  • 2005: : Awarded the Padma Shri, one of India's highest civilian honors, for his contributions to cricket.
  • 2011 : : Inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame in recognition of his illustrious career.
  • 2013 : : Appointed as the chairman of the BCCI's All-India Junior Selection Committee.
  • Present : : Continues to be involved in cricket as a commentator, mentor, and administrator, sharing his expertise and experience with the next generation of cricketers.

Viswanath's batting technique served as a blueprint for aspiring cricketers, emphasizing the importance of footwork, balance, and playing the ball late. He embodied the classical style of batsmanship, showcasing the beauty and artistry inherent in the game.
